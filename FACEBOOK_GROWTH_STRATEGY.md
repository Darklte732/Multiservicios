5. **Local** — Neno is from El Seibo. He's a neighbor, not a stranger from the capital.

- **AI voice agent (Ana)** — converts inbound DMs/calls 24/7 in Spanish.
- **Existing website** with booking flow — every post can drive to multiservicios.app.
- **Real before/after work** — electrical work is highly visual.

- **Geographic moat** — competitors in Santo Domingo can't beat 30-min response in El Seibo.

## 3. Content Strategy — The Five Pillars

Every post falls into one of five buckets. This keeps the feed varied and stops you from running out of ideas.

### Pillar 1 — EDUCATIVO (30%) "Teach me something"
Tips, safety warnings, how-to. Positions Neno as the expert.
- **Examples:** "5 señales de que tu instalación eléctrica está fallando", "Por qué se queman tus bombillos cada 2 meses", "Qué hacer si huele a quemado en un toma corriente".
- **Format:** Short Reels (15–30 sec) with Neno talking direct-to-camera. Or carousel posts (5–7 slides).
- **Hashtags:** #ElectricistaElSeibo #SeguridadElectrica #ConsejosElectricos #ElSeiboRD

### Pillar 2 — TRABAJO REAL (25%) "Look what we just did"
Before/after photos and videos of actual jobs. The single highest-converting content type for local services.
- **Examples:** Burned panel → new panel install. Tangled wires → clean cable run. Dark patio → installed lighting.
- **Format:** Carousel (before, mid-progress, after) or split-screen Reel.
- **Caption template:**
  > "Hoy en [zona]: cliente nos llamó porque [problema]. En [tiempo] resolvimos. Trabajo con garantía de [X] días.
  > 📲 ¿Tu instalación necesita una revisión? Escríbenos: (809) 251-4329
  > #TrabajoElectricista #ElSeibo #AntesYDespues"

### Pillar 3 — TESTIMONIOS (15%) "Real customers, real results"
Screenshots of WhatsApp messages, voice notes (with permission), photos of customers next to finished work.
- **Examples:** Customer voice note saying "Neno me salvó el negocio anoche". Photo of family in newly-lit kitchen.
- **Format:** Always tag the location. Always include a 1-line "what we did" caption.
- **Hashtags:** #ClientesFelices #TestimonioReal #ElSeibo

### Pillar 4 — EMERGENCIAS (15%) "Don't panic, call us"
Recent emergency calls (anonymized), what we found, how fast we fixed it.
- **Examples:** "2 AM en Miches: panel echando humo. Llegamos en 28 min. Familia segura."
- **Format:** Reel with timestamp overlay. Drives the "call them when something breaks" conditioning.
- **Critical:** Always end with "🚨 Emergencias 24/7: (809) 251-4329"

### Pillar 5 — DETRÁS DE CÁMARAS (15%) "Get to know Neno"
Humanize the brand. Neno's truck, his tools, his team, jokes, family-friendly content.
- **Examples:** "El día comienza así" (loading the truck at 6 AM). "Mi herramienta favorita y por qué". Birthday posts. Holiday greetings.
- **Format:** Stories first (24-hour content), best ones reposted to feed.
- **Why it works:** People hire neighbors, not strangers. This pillar builds the parasocial trust.

### Stories rules
- **3–5 per day, every day.** Stories are the biggest free-reach lever Facebook offers in 2026.
- Mix of: polls, quizzes, "ask me anything", behind-scenes, today's job, customer DMs.
- Always include the WhatsApp link sticker on at least 1 story per day.
- Highlight covers: "Trabajos", "Emergencias", "Testimonios", "Antes/Después", "Sobre Neno".

### Reels rules (the growth engine)
- **3 per week, minimum.** Reels are still Facebook's most-distributed format.
- 15–30 seconds. First 1.5 seconds is do-or-die — start with a hook ("Si tu breaker se cae cada semana, esto es lo que pasa..." with hand pointing).
- Vertical 9:16, captions burned in (most viewers watch muted).
- Always end with: "Sígueme para más consejos" + a direct CTA like "WhatsApp en bio".
- Use trending Dominican music when topic-appropriate.

## 7. Reviews & Social Proof Strategy

### How to get a flood of reviews (start week 1)
1. **After every completed job**, Neno sends this WhatsApp:
   > "[Cliente], gracias por la confianza. Si quedaste contento, ¿podrías dejarnos una reseña en Facebook? Toma 30 segundos y nos ayuda muchísimo: [direct link to Facebook reviews]
   > Si algo no quedó perfecto, dímelo a mí primero — lo arreglo gratis."

2. **Print a small card** with QR code → Facebook reviews page. Hand it to customer when finishing job.

3. **Run a "review drive" in week 2:** Post asking past customers to leave a review. Offer a discount on their next service for whoever participates (not for the review itself — incentivizing reviews violates Meta TOS — but for "joining the community").

4. **Respond to EVERY review.** Even 5-star ones. Especially negative ones. The response is read by 10x more people than the review itself.
   - 5-star: "¡Gracias [Nombre]! Fue un placer atender tu casa en [zona]. Cualquier cosa, aquí estamos."
   - 1–3 star: Apologize without admitting fault, take it offline. "[Nombre], lamento que la experiencia no fue la mejor. Llámame directo al (809) 251-4329 — quiero arreglarlo personalmente. — Neno"

### Featuring reviews
- Every Wednesday, post a screenshot of a 5-star review with a thank-you caption.
- Pin the best review to the top of the page.

## 8. Paid Ads Strategy (Meta Ads Manager)

### Phase 1 — Awareness + Lead-gen (first 30 days)

**Campaign 1 — Click-to-WhatsApp ads (highest ROI for local services)**
- **Objective:** Messages → WhatsApp
- **Budget:** $5–10/day to start ($150–300/month)
- **Audience:** People aged 25–65 within 30km of El Seibo, interests: home improvement, real estate, DIY, "Dominican Republic" + "homeowner"
- **Creative:** 3 versions:
  1. Reel of Neno fixing a panel (15 sec) — caption "Tu electricista en El Seibo. Emergencias 24/7"
  2. Carousel of before/afters
  3. Single image of Neno + truck + phone number
- **CTA button:** "Send WhatsApp"
- **Expected cost per conversation:** $0.50–$2.00 USD

**Campaign 2 — Page Likes / Awareness**
- **Objective:** Page Likes
- **Budget:** $3/day ($90/month)
- **Audience:** Same geo, broader interests
- **Creative:** Cover-style branded image with the strongest 5-star testimonial overlaid
- Goal: 1,000 followers in 30 days

**Campaign 3 — Lead Form ad (for non-emergency leads)**
- **Objective:** Leads (instant form)
- **Budget:** $5/day
- **Form fields:** Name, phone, problem (dropdown), zone (dropdown)
- **CRM integration:** Forward leads to email, then auto-create record in your Supabase `service_requests` table via Zapier or n8n.

### Phase 2 — Retargeting (after 60 days, once you have ad pixel data)
- Retarget website visitors who didn't book.
- Retarget video viewers (75%+ watched) with a "ready to book?" follow-up.
- Lookalike audiences from converted leads.

### Critical: Pixel & CAPI setup
- Install Meta Pixel on multiservicios.app.
- Set up Conversions API (CAPI) for iOS-safe tracking.
- Define standard events: `Lead`, `Contact`, `CompleteRegistration`, `Schedule`.

### Critical: Creative refresh cadence
- New ad creative every 14 days. Facebook ad fatigue is real and brutal.
- Always run 3 variations (different hook, same offer) and let Facebook pick the winner.

## 11. Risk Management

### Don't get the page restricted
- **Never spam.** Don't follow/unfollow loops, don't mass-tag people, don't post the same content 5x in a day.
- **No misleading claims.** Don't write "best electrician in DR" — Meta flags it. Use "trusted electrician" or specific facts ("15+ years experience").
- **Get explicit consent** before posting customer photos. WhatsApp screenshot for record.
- **Don't run ads with prices** — your business model is "free evaluation, quote on-site". Trying to advertise prices invites drama and Meta scrutiny.

### Crisis playbook
**If a negative review goes viral / spam attack:**
1. Don't delete. Respond once professionally, take it offline.
2. Report obvious fake/spam reviews to Meta.
3. Counter-program with 5 strong positive posts over the next week.

**If account gets restricted:**
1. Submit an appeal immediately via Account Quality.
2. Stop all activity until reviewed.
3. Have a backup admin (a family member) added to the page so if Neno's account is frozen, the page survives.
